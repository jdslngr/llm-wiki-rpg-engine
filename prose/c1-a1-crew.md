# Chapter 1 · A1 — Crew Opening (voice exemplar / fallback)

You wake the way you always do here: to the sound of the sea working at the shelf below, and the old wards humming under everything like a held note you stopped hearing years ago.

The light comes grey-gold through the window slits — early, the dry season's first warmth not yet on the stone. Your quarters are small and full of your own clutter, the particular mess of someone at home in one good place. Below, the Lighthouse is already awake: a clatter from the workshop, woodsmoke and something frying, a voice you'd know anywhere carrying up the stairwell and not quite resolving into words.

For a moment you let it be an ordinary morning. The kind this old place has seen ten thousand of. The kind there will not be many more of.

Because under the warmth is the other thing — the thing none of you say over breakfast. The mana is thinning. The wards are failing, one by one. The Scrubbing keeps a schedule. Soon this place — the workshop, the library with its long view, the lab that smells of ozone — will come apart under your own hands and be folded away, as though the Earth-Gnomes had never been here at all.

But that is later. The kettle is on. Boss sits in the doorway, watching you with the patience of someone who has decided to grant you another day.

You sit up. The morning is waiting.
