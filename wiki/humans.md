# Humans

## Name

At this stage in history, multiple lineages of humans exist and oftentimes interact with each other. While these groups identify primarily through tribal affiliations or deep kinship groups — referring to themselves in their various tongues as **"The People"** or **"The Living"** to distinguish themselves from the spirits and magical creatures — the Gnomes possess a more clinical, ancient perspective.

To the Gnomes, they are collectively **"The Humans,"** but many Gnomes make the distinction between the different lineages:

- **_Homo luzonensis_ & _floresiensis_:** The small-bodied, agile inhabitants of the Southeast Asian islands. Gnomes admire their ability to vanish into caves and jungle canopies. They are seen as the most "spirit-adjacent" because of their elusive nature.
- **Neanderthals:** The powerful, stocky lineages of the cold West. Gnomes respect their mastery of fire and their resilience against the encroaching ice, viewing them as the "sturdy stone" of the human family.
- **_Homo sapiens_:** The restless, high-foreheaded tribes emerging from Africa. To Gnomes, these are the most unpredictable; they possess a unique, frantic curiosity that makes them both dangerous and full of potential.
- **Denisovans:** The enigmatic, large-framed dwellers of the Asian plateaus and forests. Gnomes often find them the most thoughtful, possessing a quiet strength that mirrors the mountains they inhabit.
- **_Homo erectus_:** The "Old Guard" of the islands and plains, the last of a million-year legacy. Gnomes treat them with a certain melancholy, knowing their time on the world-stage is slowly drawing to a close.

## Location

Humans are a highly migratory collection of species. While many remain in Africa, adventurous groups have pushed deep into Southeast Asia and Europe. They are increasingly drawn to **"High-Mana" zones**, often settling on the peripheries of Gnomish sites like the [Archipelago Lighthouse](geography-and-environment.md#local-geography), where the residual warmth and light of Gnomish technology provide a temporary sanctuary from the encroaching cold and predators.

## Government Type

Their governance is **Tribal and Elders-based**, rooted in survival and tradition. Authority is typically held by those with the most experience or those who show a "knack" for interpreting the world's mysteries — such as hunters who can track magical beasts or elders who remember the songs of previous migrations. Decisions are made through a mix of survival-driven pragmatism and respect for ancestral custom, often facilitated by a central figure who acts as a bridge between the tribe and the supernatural.

## Religion / Beliefs

Human beliefs are deeply animistic and diverse, seeing the world as a vibrant, breathing web of mysteries. They do not share the Gnomish scientific view of mana; instead, they view magic as a sacred, unpredictable force inhabited by spirits.

Every group has its own creation myth shaped by their geography. Many tribes view the Gnomes as distant, terrifyingly beautiful deities or **"Star-Walkers,"** treating Gnomish ruins and Lighthouses as holy sites where the boundary between the physical and the spirit realm is thin.

## Social Structure

Human society is communal and kinship-oriented, organized into tight-knit bands where every individual's contribution — from gathering to storytelling — is vital for survival. Roles are often fluid but specialized, with a growing emergence of **"Seers"** or **"Song-Keepers"** who are the first to perceive the subconscious "nudges" from Kaspen's [Humming Spires](the-pitch.md#the-secret-mission). Status is earned through bravery, the ability to provide, and the wisdom to navigate the increasingly dangerous, low-mana environment.

## Technology Level

They are in the **Middle Stone Age**, utilizing sophisticated stone tools, prepared cores, and spears. While their technology is primitive compared to the Gnomes, they are incredibly resourceful, often scavenging **"Gnomish Waste"** — discarded alloys or thermal heat-bleeds — to enhance their tools or survive the cold. They are just beginning to experiment with complex art, such as engraved ochre and shell beads, signaling the start of a massive cognitive and cultural leap.

## Art & Culture

Art is a burgeoning, vital force for humans, used to make sense of a world they cannot control. It is expressed through storytelling, cave paintings, and rhythmic rituals. Unlike the Gnomish "Acoustic Artifice," human music is primal, utilizing drums, chanting, and dance to mimic the hum of the earth.

Their culture is one of mimicry and reverence; they weave the strange sights of Gnomish "miracles" into their folklore, creating a rich tapestry of myths that will serve as the fertile soil for Kaspen's secret seeds of inspiration.
