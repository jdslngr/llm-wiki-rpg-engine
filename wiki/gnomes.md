# Gnomes

## Name

The Gnomes are a cosmic species whose formal name for themselves is rarely used on Earth, where they are known simply as **Gnomes** — a title that will eventually pass into human legend. The Gnomes in this story are specifically known as **Earth-Gnomes**, a designation that distinguishes them from their kin in the stars and emphasizes their long-standing, deep-rooted history with the planet they are now preparing to leave behind.

## Location

Once established in cities of stone and wood across every continent, the Gnomes are now primarily concentrated at key "Scrubbing Sites" like the **[Archipelago Lighthouse](geography-and-environment.md#local-geography)**. Their footprint on Earth is rapidly shrinking as they retreat from their ancient outposts to join the majority of their population, who have already departed for the stars.

## Architecture

Gnomish architecture prioritizes underground spaces over surface structures. This approach stems from their desire for privacy and the need to stay hidden as humans become more numerous.

While they do have buildings above ground, such as the Archipelago Lighthouse (which guides Gnomish ships) and the entrances to their homes, most of their activities take place below the surface. The **"Deep-Spires"** are built underground to provide quiet environments ideal for advanced magic, experiments, and art. These spaces are chosen for their stillness and lack of disturbances.

Even with this focus on underground living, Gnomes regularly travel between their subterranean chambers and surface-level dwellings. With the exception of lighthouses, Gnomish structures, cities, and settlements are protected by strong illusions and wards, making them undetectable to outsiders. To humans, a Gnomish city looks like ordinary landscape — just a limestone bluff or a thicket. Because of these protections, no human accidentally finds a Gnomish home; their underground world remains hidden and secure, existing alongside the human world without notice.

## Government Type

The Gnomes do not have a formal, permanent government, instead operating as a **stateless, communal society** where decisions are made through collective organization and consensus.

While they recognize the **High Council** as a temporary advisory body for large-scale coordination, leadership positions are not permanent; every Gnome, at various points in their long lives, is expected to serve in a leadership or administrative role. Laws are viewed not as rigid statutes but as logical mandates agreed upon by the community.

Because Gnomes are highly intelligent and opinionated, they do not always agree; if an individual finds themselves at odds with an order, they are simply replaced by someone whose interests align with the task. When a crime or social breach occurs, there is no police force; instead, the immediate community resolves the matter, collectively devising "punishments" that are creatively tailored to the individual and the offense to restore social balance.

Without a police force or standing army, they rely on social and professional integrity for enforcement, though they remain highly capable of organizing a formidable defense if the community's safety is ever threatened.

## Religion / Beliefs

Gnomes are pragmatic. They treat magic and mana as fundamental branches of physics to be studied and mastered through the scientific method. While they do not rule out the existence of a Creator, they believe such a being is currently beyond the reach of their instruments and thus place their faith in discovery and the pursuit of knowledge.

## Social Structure

Gnomish society is fundamentally egalitarian and non-hierarchical, rooted in the belief that all members can contribute to the collective's direction. Their social fabric is built on collaboration and personal partnerships, and thrives on a diversity of thought and strong individual wills.

Status is not derived from power, rank, or wealth, but from one's ingenuity and resourcefulness. This communal pressure to conform to the group's shared goals — like the total restoration of Earth — is what makes [Kaspen's secret mission](the-pitch.md#the-secret-mission) so significant, as it represents a rare departure from the collective's transparency.

## Technology Level

As an interstellar civilization, Gnomish technology is a seamless fusion of advanced mechanical engineering and high-level magic. They have mastered **Faster-Than-Light travel** to navigate distant galaxies and utilize **"Great Workings"** to power massive infrastructure. Their mastery of **Arcane Metallurgy** and **Inscription** allows them to build automated devices and structures.

## Art & Culture

Art and culture are central to Gnomish life, serving as the primary means through which they find fulfillment across their near-immortal lifespans. With unlimited resources and vast amounts of time at their disposal, Gnomes dedicate themselves to the relentless pursuit of imagination, resulting in a society that is as much a global workshop as it is a civilization.

They view the act of creation — whether through sound, physical form, or complex magical resonance — as the highest form of expression and often engage in collaborative projects that span centuries. Beyond their own inventions, they possess a profound and curious appreciation for the art of other species, often studying and preserving foreign cultural expressions with the same scientific and aesthetic rigor they apply to their own.

Their culture values the clever, the vibrant, and the interactive, seeing every artistic endeavor as a vital contribution to the collective joy of their race.
