# Cosmology & History

## Creation Myth / Origins

*How did the universe begin?*

### Gnomish Perspective on Magic and Creation

**The Pragmatic Universe:** Most Gnomes acknowledge the universe's existence as a self-sustaining reality that likely does not require a creator.

**The Minority View:** Only a few individuals still pursue the identity of a creator or believe such a being is knowable, or that there are gods.

**Existence of Superior Entities:** Gnomes have encountered beings of immense power across the universe. While these entities are formidable and defy categorization, the Gnomes view them as extreme physical or magical phenomena rather than deities.

**Magic is Fundamental:** It's not a gift from gods but an inherent property of reality.

As an ancient race not native to Earth, the Gnomes view magic as a fundamental component of the universe. In their distant past, the Gnomes expended vast resources and intellect trying to identify the creator of reality. However, by the time this story takes place, most have abandoned this pursuit, concluding that such a truth is likely unknowable.

### Human Perspective on Magic and Creation

Human (any member of the genus *Homo*) creation myths are incredibly diverse, shaped by each group or tribe's immediate geography, climate, and relationship with nature.

Humans also possess a familiarity with supernatural forces and magic — the environment around them is perceived as brimming with mysteries. However, few humans can harness mana to perform magic; they haven't had the opportunity to study it in detail or uncover its secrets. Although intensely curious about magic, it remains an elusive and largely incomprehensible and inconsistent force to them.

Their views on magic and the supernatural are also heavily influenced by their frequent encounters with monsters, magical creatures, and strange beings.

### How Humans View Gnomes

While Gnomes have inhabited Earth for tens of thousands of years, their interaction with humans is a relatively recent development. It is only in the last thousands of years that larger numbers of human tribes have migrated near Gnomish cities and settlements.

The Gnomes are aware that their technology and magical adeptness may lead humans to view them as deities; however, they consider this a non-issue. Given their imminent departure and the upcoming Ice Age, they believe human mythologies will naturally evolve, and the memory of the Gnomes will eventually fade into insignificance. As Gnomes are near-immortal, they can appreciate time on a larger scale.

## Timeline

*A detailed chronological record, broken down into eras.*

### 200,000 BCE — Gnomes Discover Earth

When the first Gnome explorers landed, they discovered a planet inhabited by a diverse family of human species. While anatomically modern humans (*Homo sapiens*) in Africa were physically similar to humans today, they shared the Earth with several other human species.

**Technology:** Humans were using Middle Stone Age tools (prepared stone cores, spears).

**Locations of early humans:** Most *Homo sapiens* were still concentrated in Africa, though small groups were beginning to explore the Afro-Eurasian corridor. While Neanderthals and Denisovans were concentrated in Europe and Asia, older lineages like *Homo erectus* still persisted in Southeast Asia. Simultaneously, specialized relic populations like *Homo luzonensis* and *Homo floresiensis* existed across the Philippine and Indonesian island chains. *Homo sapiens* would arrive in Southeast Asia around 50,000 years ago.

**The Gnomish View:** To the Gnomes arriving in their ships, these humans would have appeared as just another sentient race — but not yet a global civilization to interact with.

### 155,000 BCE — The Era of Exploration and Building

Large fleets of Gnomes arrive. They mapped, explored, and built cities and outposts around the world. During this period, Earth was dominated by megafauna and primordial magical creatures. While the Gnomes noted the presence of early human ancestors in certain regions, they primarily lived in isolation.

### 150,000 BCE — The Observation of the "Great Fading"

Gnomish researchers discover a significant and accelerating decline in ambient mana. While the planet's surface temperature will temporarily rise toward a peak from 130,000 to 115,000 BCE (the Eemian warm period), Gnomish scientists identified that the "Arcane Insulation" of the planet was failing. They conclude that once the natural heat cycle peaked, the thinning mana would coincide with the planet entering an Ice Age where the cold would be deeper and last longer than any previous era.

Researchers are unable to find the cause and the solution, nor are they inclined to change the environment in a way that could impact human history. Recognizing that the cities will not survive a low-mana environment, the Gnomish High Council agrees to start planning for their departure from Earth. The **Pack-It-Up Protocol** — an ancient doctrine — is put into effect to ensure that as they left, no technology remained behind that could destabilize human development. Some Gnomes will start leaving as early as 145,000 BCE.

### 120,000–105,000 BCE — Human Migration and First Contact

As modern humans began to migrate and explore new territories, they increasingly crossed paths with Gnomish outposts. Small tribes began to gravitate toward Gnomish cities, drawn by the protection offered by the glow of Gnomish lighthouses.

### 105,000–100,000 BCE — The Era of Coexistence

A period of localized cooperation. While Gnomes maintained a policy of non-interference regarding human culture, they frequently defended nearby settlements from monster attacks or natural disasters. Humans became a common sight at the periphery of Gnomish society, acting as traders and witnesses to Gnomish craft. Toward the end of this period, Gnomes leave in large numbers.

### 100,000 BCE — Human Cultural Explosion

By the time the main story starts, humans were undergoing a massive shift. This is when we see the first archaeological evidence of:

- **Complex Art:** Engraved ochre and shell beads.
- **Ritual:** Explorations in magic and spirituality.
- **Long-Distance Travel:** Humans had migrated significantly into the areas where Kaspen will be planting the Humming Spires.

### 100,000 BCE–Present — The Scrubbing

The majority of the Gnomish population has already departed. The remaining Gnomes, known as the **Cleanup Crew**, are tasked with dismantling the great cities, exploring and deactivating dungeons, and scrubbing the earth of artifacts.

Stationed at the **[Archipelago Lighthouse](geography-and-environment.md#local-geography)**, Kaspen and Kaelen begin their secret mission under the watchful eye of **[Auditor Vane](main-characters.md#auditor-vane)**.

## The Great Fading and The Return of the Sun

### 100,000 BCE — The Main Story (The Final Sentinel Wave)

The "Great Fading" of mana begins to trigger a steady drop in global temperatures. Kaspen begins planting the first Humming Spires.

### 80,000–70,000 BCE — The Deepening Cold

Glaciers begin to advance from the North. The climate becomes significantly drier. Large human tribes migrate out of Africa and across the Levant, following the last corridors of warmth. Gnomish cities are now either completely dismantled or buried under shifting sands and rising ice.

### 74,000 BCE — The Toba Crisis

A massive super-volcanic eruption (Mount Toba) occurs, creating a "Volcanic Winter" that lasts for years. This event causes a severe human population bottleneck; Kaspen's Humming Spires help the survivors endure.

### 26,000–19,000 BCE — The Last Glacial Maximum (The Peak)

The Ice Age reaches its absolute harshest point. Ice sheets miles thick cover most of Northern Europe, Asia, and North America. Sea levels reach their lowest point (roughly 400 feet lower than 2026 levels), creating massive land bridges.

### 15,000 BCE — The First Pulse (The Melting)

Ambient mana levels begin a minor recharge, and global temperatures rise sharply. Vast meltwater floods reshape the landscape. Many of Kaspen's coastal Humming Spire sites are submerged by rising oceans.

### 12,800–11,700 BCE — The Younger Dryas (The Final Frost)

The Earth experiences a sudden, brief return to Ice Age conditions for about 1,000 years. This "False Winter" acts as the final test for human resilience before the permanent thaw.

### 11,700 BCE — The Great Dawn

The current warm, stable era (the Holocene) begins.

### 10,000–3,000 BCE — The Bloom of Civilization

The latent influence of the Humming Spires leads to the "spontaneous" discovery of complex musical scales, mathematics, and architectural geometry.
