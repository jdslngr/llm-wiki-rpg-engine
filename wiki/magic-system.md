# Magic System

## Overview

The magical system of this universe is defined by the presence of **mana**, a fundamental energy inherent in all living things and the environment itself. While every being possesses a "well" or internal reservoir of mana, the volume varies significantly between individuals. For both Gnomes and humans, mana functions much like a spiritual muscle; it is a finite resource that can be "spent" to manifest change in the physical world or spiritual plane, but it can also be expanded over time through consistent practice and strain training.

Most day-to-day magic — lighting a fire, mending a tool, or casting a localized spell — relies entirely on this personal supply. When a practitioner exhausts their mana reserve, they experience **Mana Fatigue**, a state of deep physical exhaustion and a loss of sensory focus.

### Ambient Mana

**Ambient mana**, by contrast, is a vast atmospheric energy that exists independently of living beings. Ambient mana is largely unusable to mages, much like sea water cannot be used in cooking food. Under normal circumstances, an individual mage does not need to influence the ambient field for standard magic.

However, ambient mana becomes essential for **"Great Workings"** — massive magical feats such as powering multiple Gnomish Lighthouses, maintaining the structural integrity of Gnomish cities, or sustaining area-wide shields and illusions. These tasks require far more energy than even a large group of mages could provide or sustain from their own bodies. As the Great Fading begins and ambient mana thins, these technologies will begin to fail.

### Gnomish Manipulation of Mana

Gnomes view mana manipulation as a versatile, scientific discipline and are capable of mastering almost any magical form. They often utilize spells and incantations as mental shortcuts to rapidly shape mana into specific outcomes, though they are equally adept at non-verbal evocation. While a Gnome's innate focus is strong, they frequently use tools like wands or staves as instruments that increase efficiency and precision.

Because Gnomes are naturally inventive, they also excel at **inscription** — anchoring mana into physical objects like the [Humming Spires](the-pitch.md#the-secret-mission) to create long-lasting, automated effects that don't require their constant attention.

### Human Manipulation of Mana

In contrast, humanity's relationship with mana is characterized by deep curiosity and a sense of mystical reverence. Although humans possess the same biological potential for magic, very few have the longevity or the academic framework to master the rapid casting common among Gnomes.

Instead, human magic is almost entirely defined by **rituals**. These are slow, rhythmic, and highly structured ceremonies involving chants, specific physical movements, or elemental catalysts. Because they cannot yet "bend" mana with a single thought, they use the structure of the ritual to slowly gather, focus, and release their energy, often mimicking the Gnomish "miracles" they may have observed.

## Rules & Limitations

1. **The Law of Magical Conservation:** Nothing is free in the universe. Magic cannot be created or lost; it can only be converted from one form to another.

2. **Limited Action at a Distance:** Magic requires a direct connection or proximity to be effective. As the distance between the caster and the target increases, the mana "leaks" and the effort required to maintain the tether becomes exponentially more difficult. Magical devices are a way of working around this limitation (e.g., long distance communication).

3. **The Sovereignty of Will:** Magic cannot override the will of another living being permanently. While a "nudge" or a creative seed can be planted, the internal Well of a sentient soul will naturally push back unless the individual is open to receiving this influence.

4. **No Resurrection:** The boundary of death is absolute. Magic can knit flesh, restart a heart, or sustain a failing body.

5. **Teleportation Constraints:** Teleportation is possible but extremely costly and limited to short distances. While Gnomes have engineered magical tools and specialized devices to extend this range, it remains a luxury for the well-equipped.

6. **Cosmic Speed Limits:** Faster-than-light travel is a reality, allowing the Gnomes to navigate the stars and reach distant galaxies. However, time travel is strictly impossible; the flow of time is a one-way street that no amount of mana can reverse.

7. **The Fog of Divination:** Divination is possible but remains very difficult and highly limited. Peering through time or space provides only fragmented, unreliable glimpses rather than clear, actionable truths.

8. **Magical By-Product:** It is extremely rare for any magical process to be perfectly efficient; unused energy from a spell is released into the immediate environment as light and/or thermal energy.

9. **The Anchor Requirement:** Magic cannot be "hung" in mid-air indefinitely; it requires a physical object or a living soul to act as a battery or conduit. Without a constant flow of mana or a physical Inscription to ground it, any active spell will naturally decay and dissolve into the ambient field.

10. **Material Conductivity:** Mana reacts differently depending on the physical substance it passes through. Using the wrong material for a wand or machine can cause the mana to bottleneck, shatter the tool, or ground out harmlessly, which is why Gnomes study arcane metallurgy so intensely.

11. **Complexity Threshold:** The more "moving parts" or layers a spell has, the more likely it is to experience a collapse. Gnomes generally prefer building physical machines to assist with complex tasks rather than relying on a single, mentally taxing super-spell.

12. **The Law of Recognition:** Magic cannot affect what the caster cannot fundamentally understand. A Gnome cannot repair a complex device or heal a specific ailment unless they have a clear mental blueprint of how that system is supposed to function.

13. **Dimensional Displacement:** Magic can fold space, but it cannot create "pocket dimensions" outside of the known universe. Any temporary space created by magic requires a constant draw on ambient mana to prevent it from snapping shut and crushing its contents.

14. **Harmonic Interference:** Two different magical effects occupying the same space can cause "Vibration Feedback," destabilizing both spells. Gnomes must carefully tune their devices — like the Humming Spires — to specific frequencies so they don't accidentally interfere with other nearby technology.

## Types of Magic

Magic in this universe is a singular force, though its application varies by culture and experience.

The **Gnomes** recognize a vast array of magical disciplines — ranging from direct evocation and complex inscription to the harmonic manipulation used in the Humming Spires — refined over eons of scientific study. Having traveled the stars, they have encountered and documented the magical practices of numerous other species, giving them a broad, academic understanding of many forms of mana manipulation in existence.

**Humans**, by contrast, are at the very beginning of their magical journey. Lacking the Gnomes' ancient archives and near-immortal lifespans, they have not yet developed formalized disciplines or an understanding of the underlying "physics" of mana. Instead, they are just starting to explore magic in their own way, primarily through trial, error, and communal rituals that interpret the supernatural through a lens of mystery rather than science.
